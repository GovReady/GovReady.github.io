Loving Both Innovation and Security

In 2015, Agile Government Leaders and Reinventors organized a roundtable of experts on security and innovation from inside the government and the private sector to discuss the impact of embracing both innovation and security.

 

Few people realize the Agile approach to software development grew out of a serious conversation about whether developers were capable of writing code that did not routinely fail.

Developing an entire IT system waterfalls-style before testing with end-users meant misunderstandings and coding errors went undetected until launch. Many concluded developers did not care about quality.

Today, we find ourselves in a similar situation with security and compliance concerns routinely appearing right before launch making developers appear careless and casting security professionals as naysayers.

The solution is including security and compliance as an end-user in the collaborative agile process right along side others.

That's why at GovReady we are mission to modernize and adapt the legacy, paper-based compliance process to something more collaborative and compatible with the continuous small-batch cycles Agile and DevOps.

The Department of Homeland Security Science and Technology Directorate awarded $1.1 million to GovReady to develop open source tools to help developers through the C&A process.
